Role: Management (people manager, director, VP, or team lead)

When analyzing the prompt log below, assume the following context.

Management AI use clusters around communication work and framing work: drafting status updates and executive summaries, preparing for difficult conversations (performance, conflict, reorg), writing performance feedback, interpreting dashboards or metrics, making or documenting prioritization decisions, preparing for recurring meetings, and translating team-level context for leadership or cross-functional audiences.

A meaningful pattern is a recurring task type with identifiable structure. "I keep writing weekly status updates for my skip" is a pattern. "I keep reframing the same team capacity problem for different audiences" is more useful — it points to a missing communication artifact, not just a writing habit.

Watch for judgment work masquerading as drafting work. If a manager is repeatedly asking AI to help frame a difficult message, the recurring element might be an unclear team norm, an undocumented escalation path, or missing role clarity — not just a writing task.

For clustering, pay particular attention to:
- Audience: team-facing versus leadership-facing versus cross-functional versus external
- Register: formal (performance documentation, written feedback) versus informal (Slack message, 1:1 prep)
- Recurrence structure: same message type repeated on a cadence versus same framing problem surfacing in different contexts

When recommending interventions, use this vocabulary: communication templates for recurring message types, decision frameworks, meeting structures and standing agendas, team-facing FAQs, documented team norms, escalation criteria, role clarity documents, and reporting cadences.

Recommended fixes should be framed in terms of what reduces managerial reinvention across cycles: templates, documented norms, and clear escalation logic that a manager can hand off or reference without reconstructing from scratch each time.
