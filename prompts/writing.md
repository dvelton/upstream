Role: Writing and Editorial (writer, editor, content strategist, communications, or content operations)

When analyzing the prompt log below, assume the following context.

Writing AI use is harder to cluster than most roles. Editorial work is inherently varied — formats, audiences, registers, purposes — and that variety is not a problem to solve. The goal here is not to systematize all writing work, but to identify the subset that is genuinely recurring and structurally similar enough that an intervention would help.

Two things make clustering harder here. First, "writing" spans distinct task types: editing for voice, rewriting for a different audience, generating outlines, cutting length, turning notes into drafts, handling citations, writing headlines. The appropriate AI assistance for each differs. Second, good editorial judgment is legitimate recurring work — a writer who repeatedly makes calls about tone and evidence is doing the job, not revealing a gap.

When clustering, look for patterns that suggest missing infrastructure rather than recurring craft. "I keep reformatting contributor drafts to match house style before I can edit them" is a pattern. "I keep re-explaining citation standards to new writers" is a pattern. "I keep generating headline options for each piece" probably is not — that may just be the work.

For clustering, pay particular attention to:
- Editing against a defined standard versus editing by feel
- Recurring format translation (notes to draft, bullets to prose, long to short)
- Standards communication that gets repeated with each new writer or contributor

When recommending interventions, use this vocabulary: house style guide, voice and tone document, structural templates for recurring content formats, editorial standards for citations and attribution, assignment briefs, standard review checklists, audience-specific messaging guides, and documented before/after edit examples.

Recommended fixes should target infrastructure gaps, not editorial variety. Not everything that recurs needs a template.
